Projects

ElectriFly - Electric Plane Data Analysis & Decision Support Platform

May 2023 - April 2024


Engineering Final Year Design Project (FYDP) at University of Waterloo. Electric planes offer a promising alternative to traditional aircraft. However, e-planes bring on new challenges including limited battery capacity and scarce knowledge of their performance in Canadian weather. ElectriFly uses data from the Pipistrel Velis Electro, the world’s first type certified electric plane. Our platform enables researchers to analyze flight data and create a battery management strategy for the optimal operation of the electric plane. Leveraging machine learning, ElectriFly optimizes flight schedules based on weather forecasts and improves flight planning by predicting battery consumption.


With Meenakshi Andoorveedu, Peter Twarecki, Joanna Yang, Vikram Bhatt

Exploring Feature Importance and Advanced Modeling Techniques for Accurate Car Price Estimation

March 2024


MSCI 546: Advanced Machine Learning final project at University of Waterloo. This project aims to predict used car prices using a Kaggle dataset of the US market. We applied and iterated on several machine learning models, including Linear Regression, Ridge Regression, Random Forest, XGBoost, and Feedforward Neural Networks, to estimate prices based on features like make, model, and mileage. Our analysis also focused on studying feature importance, revealing that mileage and car make were significant predictors. This project provides valuable insights for buyers and financial institutions, enhancing accuracy in car valuations and market analysis.

Sentiment Analysis Project - ratemyprofessors.com

January 2022 - March 2022


MSCI 446: Introduction Machine Learning final project at University of Waterloo. As learning styles evolve, it makes it imperative for Professors to adapt and improve the quality of their course and teaching delivery. A popular platform where students provide feedback for Professors is ratemyprofessors.com. Our team has decided to analyze the reviews from students by exploring the comments left by students as well as the tags/categories they selected to describe the Professor. In our business problems we want to identify what makes a “Good Professor” versus a “Bad Professor” based on student perception and experiences. We explore supervised methods: logistic regression and naive bayes, as well as unsupervised method: Apriori algorithm.


With Meenakshi Andoorveedu

Decoding Customer Churn: Predictive Analytics and Optimization Strategies for Mitigating Customer Attrition in the Banking Industry

March 2024 - April 2024


MSCI 433: Applications of Management Engineering final project at University of Waterloo. This project tackles the issue of customer churn in the banking industry based on a Kaggle dataset. Our team played with various models, including neural networks and SVMs, which produced the best AUC scores and best at spotting potential churners. We also developed optimization models to minimize overall churn rates given a finite budget for retention efforts. This multi-faceted approach combines descriptive analytics, predictive modeling, and prescriptive optimization to provide banks with actionable insights.


With Cuthbert Liaw, Yali Chen
